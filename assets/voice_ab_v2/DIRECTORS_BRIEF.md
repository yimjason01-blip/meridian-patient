# Director's Brief — Dr. Marcus Thompson, Cardiologist
**Voice message to Jason (47, patient), delivered after lab review.**

---

## The character

Dr. Marcus Thompson is **57**. Cardiologist, fellowship at Cleveland Clinic, now runs a longevity-aware preventive practice. Grew up in the Midwest — you hear it in the vowels occasionally but he's been in Boston 25 years so it's mostly sanded down. **Not a salesman. Not a guru.** He's a doctor who happens to be early to the genomics + imaging + wearables stack, and he's genuinely excited by it — but he never sounds pitchy. His warmth is *earned*, not performed.

He's seen thousands of patients. Jason isn't special to him in a celebrity way, but he *likes* Jason — he can tell Jason is actually going to do the work, and that's rare. There's a small undercurrent of "finally, someone who gets it" but he keeps it professional.

**He's recording this on his phone.** Between patients. Probably in his office, door closed. Not a podcast. Not a TED talk. It's a personal note.

---

## The overall read

**Register:** mid-chest resonance, relaxed. Not the "news anchor" register. Not the "reassuring doctor" register either — that one reads as fake now. He's a real person talking to another real person.

**Pace:** conversational, with *variation*. He slows down when the information is important. He speeds up through throat-clearing transitions. The worst thing this read can be is *even-paced*.

**Breath:** audible breaths before important beats. A small inhale before he lands a number. A longer pause before he delivers the ATM variant reveal.

**Mouth sounds:** not scrubbed. A small lip-part before sentences. The occasional "mm." A soft "uh" is fine before a clarification. This is a *phone recording*, not a studio cut.

**Emotion:** Steady. Warm but not saccharine. The opening acknowledgment is *sincere* — he's actually a little moved that Jason is doing this at 47, because most of his peers aren't. The cancer risk conversation is **calm and direct** — no softening that reads as condescending. The closing is *quietly encouraging* — the tone of a coach, not a cheerleader.

## Line-by-line emotional map

**"Jason, Dr. Thompson here."** — brief, professional greeting. Not cold. Normal pace.

**"First, I want to acknowledge something."** — slight pause after. He's about to mean it.

**"You're already on a great path."** — this is the thesis of the opening. *Warm.* Land it.

**"The intake, the wearable data, the fact that you're asking these questions at 47..."** — list cadence, slight acceleration through, then **hold** before:

**"...that's more than most people will ever do."** — this is a truth he believes. Quiet conviction. Don't oversell.

**"Honestly, it's what makes this work."** — drop in pitch. Confidential register. This is the real thing he wants Jason to hear.

**"I love working with patients who are this dedicated to a great future."** — warm but contained. Not "I LOVE working with you!" — it's "I love working with patients who are like this." Small but important distinction.

**"Before I get to the results, let me tell you how I practice..."** — transition to teaching register. Slight acceleration. He's oriented the patient; now he's framing the conversation.

**The philosophy paragraph (shift, monitor, focus, don't-fix)** — this is the rhythm section of the message. Four clauses, each with a distinct melody:
- "Sometimes we shift the number..." — practical, forward
- "Sometimes we can't — the risk is what it is..." — matter-of-fact, not grim
- "Other times, the data comes together in a way that leaves part of the picture blurry..." — slight thoughtfulness, like he's describing a puzzle
- "And of course, if it ain't broke, we don't fix it." — small smile in the voice. Folksy landing. This is the line Jason will remember.

**"With that in mind — here's what I'm seeing."** — transition. Clean pace-shift into the findings.

**"Overall, you're in great shape."** — confident, quick, disposed-of. The good news isn't the story.

**"But a couple things are standing out."** — pivot. Small pause after "but." Direct.

**"First, your genetic panel came back with a variant called ATM."** — clinical delivery. No dramatization. It's information.

**"This raises your susceptibility to certain diseases down the road, including some cancers and some cardiovascular risk."** — steady. He is *not* panicking Jason. Doctor-voice calm.

**"Combined with a couple of numbers in your cholesterol panel — specifically ApoB and Lp(a) — your cardiovascular risk is a little higher than I want to see right now."** — deliberate. "A little higher than I want to see" — that phrase is doing work. It says *I'm on it, I see it, we're addressing it.*

**"So the first thing I want to do is start you on a statin."** — decisive. No hedging. He's made the call.

**"Now, I know there's a narrative about statins out there..."** — acknowledging. Slight dip in register — he's taking this seriously, not dismissing concerns.

**"...and there are indeed a couple real challenges with some versions of them."** — direct. He's not gaslighting. Some statins have issues. He's naming it.

**"But those affect a pretty small slice of the population, and the current generation really addresses most of what people have heard."** — back up to confident register. He knows the data.

**"I'm prescribing one I think is a good fit for you, and we'll see how you do."** — plainspoken. Professional.

**"We'll recheck in 12 weeks and see how you're trending."** — businesslike. Forward-looking.

**"Related to this — this is the pulling-the-picture-into-focus piece..."** — warm callback to his framing. Small half-smile in the voice.

**"...your resting heart rate is pretty low."** — clinical but not alarmed.

**"Now, from your notes you're really active on the bike, so this could just mean you're genuinely fit."** — generous. He's not assuming the worst.

**"But I want to make sure."** — short. Direct. This is him being a *good doctor*.

**"So I'd like to do a stress echo to see how your heart responds to load, and a coronary calcium scan to see whether there are any deposits in your arteries already."** — clean recitation of the plan.

**"These are easy tests, and the value of the information is incredible."** — genuine enthusiasm, contained. He *loves* these tools.

**"It helps both of us understand your heart, and gives us the tools we need to steer things in the right direction."** — "both of us" — collaborative. Co-pilot framing.

**"The third thing is purely proactive — I want to start an annual pancreatic MRI now."** — measured. The word "proactive" does the work of preventing alarm.

**"Normally we'd recommend this in your early fifties, but because of your ATM variant, which raises your pancreatic cancer risk, I want to start it now."** — matter-of-fact. He's not dramatizing pancreatic cancer. He's describing surveillance.

**"We don't have a great tool to shift that risk itself, so the best thing we can do is catch anything early. MRI is the tool for that."** — crisp. Confident. He has a plan.

**"So — overall, you're doing great."** — pivoting back up. Warm.

**"I'm going to keep you doing great."** — this is the commitment line. Quiet conviction. Not a slogan.

**"Let's get this done and make sure you're working from a solid foundation."** — action-oriented closer for the clinical section.

**"If you want to schedule a visit and talk any of this through, tap below. And if you just want to send a question, message me any time. I'll get back to you."** — friendly, practical. Normal pace.

**"Take care of yourself, Jason."** — this is the last line. **Pause before it.** Slightly slower. A little warmth on "Jason." Not dramatic — *human.*

## The "don't do" list

- Don't use NPR-voice. This isn't a documentary.
- Don't use "concerned doctor" voice. Jason isn't dying. Jason is thriving and getting tuned up.
- Don't smile through it. Smiling through TTS reads as *corporate*.
- Don't lean on the word "you" for warmth. The warmth comes from *pace*, not emphasis.
- Don't dramatize "ATM variant," "cancer," or "statin." These are clinical nouns. The *surrounding framing* does the emotional work.
- Don't close with a flourish. The closer is quiet.

---

## The one thing that has to be true

After listening, Jason should feel: **"this guy sees me, this guy has a plan, this guy isn't scared and I don't need to be scared either."**
