Start a low-dose statin (rosuvastatin 10 mg nightly)

Because your ApoB and Lp(a) together put cardiovascular risk materially above your age band — and this is the single biggest dial to move.
What the plan is Take one 10 mg pill at bedtime. No food requirement. Side effects to watch: unusual muscle soreness — tell us if it happens.
Why it matters for you specifically ApoB 102 mg/dL (goal <80) and Lp(a) elevated. Together these are driving most of your cardiac risk trajectory. This intervention alone moves your 10-year risk from 11.2% to roughly 7.6%.
How we'll know it's working Repeat ApoB at 12 weeks. Target: <80 mg/dL. You'll also likely see your LDL drop 40%+.

8-week sleep and stress protocol

Because your HRV has been trending down for 6 months and your resting heart rate crept up. That's a nervous-system story, and it's likely making the cardiac number worse.
What the plan is Extend sleep to 7.5 hours as a minimum. Wind-down 30 minutes before bed — no screens, dim lights, room below 68°F. Breath work protocol 10 min/day (we'll send the specific one). Reassess in 8 weeks.
Why it matters for you specifically HRV dropped from 58ms (Oct) to 42ms (now). Resting HR rose from 52 to 58. This is the autonomic pattern that amplifies inflammatory and cardiovascular risk simultaneously.
How we'll know it's working HRV back above 50ms on 4-week average. Resting HR under 55.

Cardiac workup before endurance training spikes

Because you're pushing hard on the bike — we want a clean look at the coronary arteries before we let the intensity climb further.
What the plan is One-time coronary calcium scan (CAC) + stress echocardiogram. Both are low-risk, done outpatient in ~90 minutes total. Scheduling handled by our office.
Why it matters for you specifically At 47 with elevated ApoB/Lp(a) and a family history of cardiac disease, we want a baseline before the statin has time to remodel anything. It's the most durable data point we'll ever get on your arteries.
How we'll know it's working Result itself is the answer. If CAC < 100 we stay the course; if higher we revisit intensity targets.
