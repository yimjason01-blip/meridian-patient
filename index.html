Baseline PSA — your ATM-driven prostate surveillance

As an ATM carrier you should have started this at 40. You're seven years overdue. It's a single blood test plus a brief exam.
What it isOne blood draw (PSA) plus a brief in-office exam (DRE). Five minutes total. Result back in 1–2 days.
Why it matters for youATM heterozygous carriers have ~2× lifetime prostate cancer risk and a higher-grade phenotype. Catching anything early is the entire game — late-stage prostate cancer survival is dramatically worse than early-stage. This sets your annual cadence.
How we'll know it's workingThe result itself is the answer. Normal PSA confirms you're on the right cadence; we recheck annually. Anything elevated triggers urology follow-up.

Start a low-dose statin (rosuvastatin 5 mg → 10 mg nightly)

Your Lp(a) is high. It's lifetime, genetically determined, and lifestyle won't move it. A statin lowers the modifiable side of the equation.
What it isOne pill at bedtime. Start at 5 mg for four weeks, then 10 mg. No food requirement. Watch for unusual muscle soreness — tell us if it happens.
Why it matters for youLp(a) 62 mg/dL (≈149 nmol/L), ApoB 98, LDL 128. Lp(a) is genetic and won't move with diet or exercise — but a statin drops every other atherogenic particle, which matters more when Lp(a) is high, not less.
How we'll know it's workingRepeat ApoB and full lipid panel at 6–8 weeks. Target ApoB <80 mg/dL. Your LDL should drop ~40%.
